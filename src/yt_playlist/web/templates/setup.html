1 · Capture your sign-in

Why sign in this way?

YouTube Music has no public, official API for managing your library and playlists. The published YouTube Data API doesn't expose them, and there's no official OAuth flow for the internal endpoints the app actually uses. So instead of OAuth, we reuse the signed-in request your own browser already makes (the Copy as cURL below), which carries the cookies and headers those endpoints expect. This is the same approach established tools like ytmusicapi take, out of necessity.

Your credentials are stored locally and only ever sent to YouTube, never to us or any third party.

What does this give access to?

The capture is your Google sign-in itself: the session cookies (SID, SAPISID, …) your browser sends to YouTube Music, plus the matching authorization header. These are Google account-level credentials, not a YouTube-only token: the same cookies sign you in across Google. There is no narrower, YouTube-scoped credential to capture, which is exactly why there's no OAuth option. Treat the saved file like a password. This is why the app runs locally: we cannot host a service that captures these.

In practice the app only uses it to read and manage your YouTube Music library and playlists, and it's built so it can't quietly do more. A built-in network guard restricts the app to YouTube and a handful of music-metadata services, blocking anything else before it leaves your machine; you can watch every request it makes on the Network page (full details in the privacy policy). To revoke the sign-in entirely, sign out of YouTube Music or remove the session from your Google account security page; that invalidates the saved capture.

  1. Open music.youtube.com signed in, then open DevTools (F12) → Network.
  2. Click any playlist (e.g. in the left sidebar). That fires a browse request. Type browse in the filter and click that browse request row.
  3. Right-click it → Copy → Copy as cURL (Windows: Copy as cURL (bash)), then paste below.

2 · Your identities

One row per YouTube identity. Exactly one is the master: your main account, where cross-identity merges land (we fill its label in once your sign-in checks out). A brand identity also needs its brand_account_id: open myaccount.google.com/brandaccounts, pick the account, and copy the id from the URL …/b/<id>/. Leave it blank for your main account.
